What?

This week, the focus shifted to designing and querying relational databases with discipline. Our challenge was to define a small set of related tables for a fictional application, ensuring proper use of primary and foreign keys. We also had to write secure CRUD operations in SQL — a Create, a Read, and an Update or Delete query — each with clarity and intent.

So What?

This task helped me appreciate the importance of structure and foresight in database design. Seeing data relationships visualized in real time emphasized how careless logic or bloated queries can degrade both performance and security. Good databases aren’t just about storing data — they’re about managing it responsibly, anticipating future maintenance and scalability.

Now What?

I plan to deepen my SQL skills by practicing more complex queries and studying indexing and normalization strategies. I also want to focus on writing queries that are efficient, safe from injection attacks, and easy for others to understand. My goal is to treat every database interaction as a shared responsibility, not just a technical task.
